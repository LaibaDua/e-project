The Big five
American Alligator: The American Alligator (Alligator mississippiensis) is a large reptile native to the southeastern United States. It is typically found in freshwater environments like rivers, lakes, and swamps. Males can grow up to 13-15 feet long, while females are smaller, reaching around 9-10 feet.
American Crocodile: The American Crocodile (Crocodylus acutus) is a large reptile found in coastal areas of the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida, as well as parts of Central and South America and the Caribbean.
Florida Panther: The Florida Panther (Puma concolor coryi) is a subspecies of cougar found primarily in the forests and swamps of southern Florida, USA. It is a medium-sized wild cat, with males typically weighing between 120-160 pounds and females around 60-100 pounds.
Bottlenose Dolphin: The Bottlenose Dolphin (Tursiops truncatus) is one of the most well-known and widely distributed species of dolphin. They are found in warm and temperate seas worldwide, including coastal areas and offshore waters.
Roseate Spoonbill The Roseate Spoonbill (Platalea ajaja) is a striking wader bird found in coastal habitats, such as wetlands, mangroves, and mudflats, throughout the Americas, particularly in the southeastern United States, Central America, and parts of South America.

Mammals
MANATEE: The manatee is a large, slow-moving aquatic mammal, also known as the sea cow, that inhabits warm coastal waters and feeds primarily on seagrass.
RACCOON: The raccoon is a nocturnal, omnivorous mammal known for its distinctive black "mask" of fur around its eyes and its dexterous front paws.
WHITE-TAILED DEER: The White-tailed Deer is a medium-sized herbivorous mammal native to North America, recognized by its white underside on its tail, which it displays when alarmed.
MARSH RABBIT: The Marsh Rabbit is a small, nocturnal mammal native to wetlands in the southeastern United States, often found in marshes and along riverbanks.
RIVER OTTER: The River Otter is a playful, semi-aquatic mammal known for its agility in water, often found in rivers, lakes, and wetlands across North America.





Birds
PEREGRINE FALCON : The Peregrine Falcon is a powerful bird of prey known for its incredible speed, capable of reaching over 240 miles per hour during its hunting dive.
GREAT BLUE HERON: The Great Blue Heron (Ardea herodias) is a large wading bird native to North and Central America, found in a variety of wetland habitats, including lakes, rivers, marshes, and coastal areas.
ANHINGA: The Anhinga (Anhinga anhinga), also known as the "snakebird" or "water turkey," is a large water bird native to the Americas, found in freshwater habitats such as lakes, rivers, and wetlands.
WOOD STORK: The Wood Stork (Mycteria americana) is a large wading bird native to the southeastern United States, Central America, and parts of South America.
SNAIL KITE: The Snail Kite (Rostrhamus sociabilis) is a medium-sized raptor found in freshwater wetlands across parts of the Americas, particularly in southern Florida, Central America, and parts of South America.
